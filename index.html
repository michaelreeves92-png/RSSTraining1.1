Introduction & Role Overview

Document Confidentiality Notice

The contents of this document are confidential and should not be shared with anyone other than the intended recipient. This training provides specific instruction to Retail Security Specialists (RSS) working in high-loss Coles stores as part of the Loss Levels Program.

Position Overview

The Retail Security Specialist (RSS) is a dedicated, full-time (40 hours/week) security position responsible for managing crime-related loss in high-loss retail stores and assisting with providing a safe environment for team members. This position serves as a critical link between store operations, security systems, and law enforcement to create a comprehensive loss prevention strategy while ensuring the safety and security of all personnel.

Key Objective: The RSS role supports Coles' business strategy of providing excellence in customer service with a targeted approach to loss controls and team member safety.

Team Integration

RSS officers work collaboratively with multiple teams to create a comprehensive security network:

Intelligence Operations Centre (IOC)

Real-time monitoring and alert triage support, CCTV oversight, and intelligence coordination. Note: IOC support is not currently available at all RSS locations.

Centre Security & Management

Shopping centre security teams and management for coordinated responses and incident management.

Uniformed Security Officers

Visible security presence and support during incidents and high-risk periods.

Coles Team Members

Store staff, managers, and customer service teams providing operational support.

Local Police

External law enforcement for incident response and investigation support.

Core Duties & Responsibilities

1

Security Presence and Response

Maintain a visible overt security presence on the shop floor as a licensed security professional. Respond to all loss technology alerts including wrong way detection, weighted shelves, Veesion, Move Alert, and ANPR systems.

2

Incident Management & Team Safety

Respond to and manage safety incidents in real-time, including collating witness statements, police evidence, and situation reports. Assist with providing a safe working environment for all team members by maintaining security presence and responding to threats or aggressive behaviour directed at staff.

3

Police and External Relationships

Build and maintain local police relationships, serving as the primary store contact. Report theft/security incidents to police within established reporting parameters and establish relationships with center management and security teams.

4

Evidence Collection and Documentation

Review CCTV footage and upload evidence to Auror reporting system. Collect evidence including receipts, photos, and other documentation. Compile and organise evidence required by police to charge offenders.

5

Technology Monitoring

Monitor and respond to all loss technology alerts in partnership with the IOC. Maximise the benefit of security technologies through real-time intervention and identify high-harm offenders as they offend.

Code of Conduct & Professional Standards

Fundamental Policy Statement

MASG HAS A STRICT "NO FRATERNISATION" POLICY
RSS officers must conduct themselves professionally at all times. Any unnecessary social interaction with Coles team members, management, and/or customers, in person or on any social media platform will not be tolerated.

Professional Standards

Foundation Principle

RSS officers must build a positive, respectful working environment with Coles Team Members by maintaining professional work-related conversations only. Personal relationships, social interactions, and non-work communications are strictly prohibited.

Professional Demeanor Requirements

You must always maintain professional demeanour as a representative of MA Services and Coles:

  • Always be positive, courteous, professional, and proactive
  • Present oneself with utmost professionalism, including correct uniform
  • Never discriminate, harass, or disparage another person
  • Never act in a manner that causes discredit to yourself, your team, or the Coles and MA Services brand

Honesty and Integrity Standards

Zero Tolerance Policy

MASG will not allow any dishonesty or malpractice from RSS officers. This includes any unlawful, unethical, or improper behaviour that may cause damage, loss, reputational damage, or loss of goodwill.

Examples of Dishonest Behaviour (Prohibited)

Leaving early without authorisation
Taking extended lunch breaks beyond entitlements
Assisting another in the commission of theft, fraud, or misconduct
Any behaviour that damages the reputation of MA Services or Coles
Misrepresenting activities or incident details
Falsifying reports or documentation

Investigation and Disciplinary Action

If an RSS officer is suspected of engaging in dishonest behaviour, an investigation will be launched. If evidence supports the allegation, formal disciplinary action will be taken, which may result in termination of employment and/or police involvement.

Anti-Harassment Policy

Definition

Harassment is any unwelcome behaviour that offends, humiliates, or intimidates a person and creates an unpleasant work environment. All forms of harassment are strictly prohibited.

Verbal Harassment Examples

  • Sexual comments, advances or propositions
  • Repeated questions about personal life
  • Indecent jokes or innuendos
  • Racist comments or spreading false information
  • Comments about disability, pregnancy, sexual orientation, age, or religion
  • Offensive language or inappropriate contact

Non-Verbal Harassment Examples

  • Leering or inappropriate staring
  • Posting unwanted material on social media platforms
  • Invasion of private space or standing too close unnecessarily
  • Mimicking someone with a disability
  • Inappropriate gestures or displays

Physical Harassment Examples

  • Unwelcome physical contact (kissing, hugging, pinching, patting, touching)
  • Indecent or sexual assault or attempted assault
  • Hitting, pushing, shoving, spitting, or throwing objects
  • Blocking someone's path or cornering

Important Social Media Reminders

Core Customer Engagement Principles

😊 Smile and Say "Hello" When Customers Enter

Our goal is to ensure a wonderful shopping experience at any retail store for all customers. We do this by making the store a pleasant place to be. Greet people with positivity and always treat them with respect. Remember, most customers are genuine and there to shop.

🚫 Never Chase a Customer or Shoplifter

At no stage do we chase a shoplifter or suspected shoplifter; chasing a shoplifter puts your safety as well as other customers' safety in great risk. Chasing a shoplifter does not at all create a welcoming and friendly environment.

💬 Keep Conversations Professional

Maintaining a good working relationship with the client is important, however refrain from making personal comments or compliments (e.g., about their appearance). You may think you are being friendly, however in most cases this can actually be deemed harassment.

🤚 Never Touch a Customer

Remember: Do Not Use Force to stop a person or Try to Forcibly recover items. Physically engaging with a potential shoplifter will greatly increase your risk of being assaulted and will put other customers and staff at risk. Also consider the brand damage that could be caused due to a physical incident.

😊 Smile When a Customer Leaves

We should always end the customer's experience on a positive note. Remember we want them to shop again, the customer after all is the reason why we are employed. We talk with customers in a professional and courteous manner because we LOVE what we do and we are PASSIONATE about helping our client achieve their goals.

Communication De-escalation Skills

Fundamental Principle

There is an equal and opposite reaction to every action!
RSS officers who communicate with persons of interest with discretion and courtesy are less likely to be assaulted. By utilising excellent communication skills and treating individuals with respect, you will have a better chance of obtaining greater cooperation and are less likely to attract verbal or physical abuse.

De-escalation Communication Principles

Be respectful at all times, regardless of the situation
Speak in a friendly manner using a level below your normal speaking voice
Use rapport techniques to build connection and trust
Ensure your tone is calm and non-threatening throughout the interaction
Think before you speak - consider the impact of your words
Do not call them a thief, criminal or otherwise - refer only to "items not paid for"
Enter conversation to distract them from the issue at hand rather than confront
Treat persons of interest as you would want to be treated - with respect

DETER Framework for Person of Interest Management

Framework Purpose

DETER provides a process for engaging with and managing Persons of Interest once the decision has been made to act. RSS officers should use this process as a mental checklist before, during and after an engagement. The main aim of DETER is to provide an overwhelming level of positive and proactive customer service to prevent unwanted activity before it occurs.

D

Decide

Assess the customer. If there is sufficient evidence to suggest a criminal offence is about to be committed, make a decision to act appropriately. Ensure you have reasonable grounds before proceeding.

E

End Result

Establish an ideal outcome from the beginning and work towards that outcome with assistance of team members. Focus on prevention rather than confrontation.

T

Tools/Teamwork

Use headsets to communicate with team members. Request attendance to utilise customer service skills. Be specific about what you want team members to do.

E

Engage

RSS officers engage the person of interest while inside the store. Approach the aisle where person is located, engage other customers first where possible, then engage the person of interest.

R

Record/Report

Always record observations and interactions. Ensure significant incidents are properly reported through management and documented in required systems.

Critical Safety Note

When the person of interest is displaying signs of possible aggression, do not request team members to assist - only RSS officers or store management may assist. If aggression continues, provide a clear pathway for exit and report the incident immediately.

Important Communication Reminders

Language and Approach Guidelines

  • Do not call customers "thieves," "criminals," or make direct accusations
  • Refer to items as "not paid for" rather than "stolen"
  • Use rapport-building techniques to maintain positive interactions
  • Enter conversations to distract from potential theft rather than confront
  • Treat all persons of interest as you would want to be treated - with respect

Technology Systems & Loss Prevention

Technology Integration Overview

RSS officers must understand and respond to various technology systems designed to detect and prevent theft. These systems work in partnership with the IOC to provide real-time alerts and intervention opportunities.

Loss Prevention Technology Systems

Wrong Way Detection

Function: Camera analytics tracking wrong way exit through entry gates
Benefits: Provides real-time alerts for intervention and bookmarked footage for future review
Response: Immediate assessment and appropriate customer engagement

Weighted Shelves

Function: Weight-based technology detecting and deterring theft
Benefits: Delivers SMS alerts to team members paired with audio alerts
Response: Multiple prevention instances through real-time monitoring

Veesion

Function: Computer vision AI detecting potential theft down aisles
Benefits: Real-time alerts triaged by IOC (currently in proof-of-concept)
Response: IOC coordination for assessment and intervention

Move Alert

Function: Infrared technology above high-risk areas
Benefits: Audio-only alerts requiring no team member interaction
Response: Focused on vitamins category with significant loss reduction results

CCTV Systems

CCTV Management Requirements

  • All CCTV uploaded to Auror should be exported from Coles CCTV system
  • IOC can assist with obtaining CCTV footage when needed
  • Ensure proper chain of custody for all video evidence

Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS)

EAS System Overview

EAS gates are commonly seen across the front of stores and appear as transparent, shoulder-height panels with red lights. Some are built into floors or checkouts and are more discreet. Their purpose is to detect security tags in high-value products as they exit the store.

1

EAS Activation Response

RSS officers are required to respond to any activation they see or hear. Always approach with positive customer service mindset.

2

Customer Service Approach

Provide proactive/positive customer service. Many activations are accidental due to incorrect deactivation or pre-existing purchases.

3

Smart EAS Features

Smart EAS gates in ACO areas make discreet announcements when live tags enter and will alarm if tags are still live when exiting ACO area.

Critical EAS Understanding

AN EAS ACTIVATION IS NOT PROOF OF THEFT
Many activations are caused by incorrect deactivation at point of sale or items already purchased before entering the store. Always approach with customer service mindset, not accusatory behaviour.

Smart Systems

Smart Gates Operation

Smart Gates are located in ACO (Automated Checkout) areas and open after payment completion. Important: Coles ACO/Customer Service staff manage manual operations. RSS officers do NOT assist with gate management, and MA guards are NOT provided with remotes.

High Risk Product Lines

Organised Retail Crime (ORC) Focus

RSS officers must focus attention on ORC target items, which are stolen in large quantities for resale. Understanding these products helps prioritise monitoring efforts.

Primary High-Risk Items

  • Meat products (fresh and packaged)
  • Baby Formula (all brands and types)
  • Dental products (electric toothbrushes, premium brands like Sensodyne)
  • Razor blades (premium brands/versions)
  • Vitamins and Medicines (high value, small packet types)
  • Health & Beauty products (makeup and skin care products)
  • Confectionary (bulk theft common)

Secondary High-Risk Items (Store Dependent)

  • Coffee (premium brands)
  • Cooking Oil
  • Batteries
  • Small Appliances / USB accessories
  • Soft Drinks (bulk theft)

Store-Specific Intelligence

Every store is different. RSS officers should speak with Store Management to ascertain current high loss areas/items, methods being employed by offenders, and any known recidivist offenders frequenting the store.

Common Methods of Theft Recognition

Detection Skills Development

RSS officers must become highly aware of theft methods to enable effective detection of persons of interest. Understanding these patterns helps with early intervention and prevention.

Concealment Methods

  • Personal Items: Inside handbags, backpacks, or personal shopping bags
  • Body Concealment: Inside clothing, jackets, or pants
  • Trolley/Basket: Hidden among other shopping or in trolley compartments
  • Package Manipulation: Removing items from packaging while in store

Exit Strategies

  • Bulk Loading: Loading trolleys with large quantities and leaving via main entrance
  • Self-Service Manipulation: Not scanning items at checkout or partial scanning
  • Avoidance Patterns: Using main entrance to avoid team members or security officers
  • Quick Exit: Rapid departure after selection and concealment

Event Reporting Decision Framework

Incident and Offender Identification Framework

Important Clarification: "Identification" refers to who first spots or recognises the incident or offender. This determines the reporting pathway and responsibility allocation for documentation, evidence collection, and external notifications.

Critical Understanding: Two Event Types

Non-Emergency Events: Standard theft, prevention, suspicious activity, routine security matters

Emergency Events: Immediate danger, violence, weapons, serious threats, incidents requiring urgent police response

The same person who identifies (spots) the incident determines the workflow, but responsibilities differ significantly between emergency and non-emergency situations.

NON-EMERGENCY EVENT REPORTING WORKFLOWS

RSS

When RSS/Covert Officer Spots Incident/Offender

RSS Officer Responsibilities:

  • Auror Report: Create complete Auror event with all incident details
  • IOC Communication: Tag Auror group "IOC" in comments section
  • Evidence Collection: Collect training receipts, photograph products, document physical evidence
  • Statement Generation: Write formal statement as the officer directly involved in incident
  • Police Reporting: Report to police once footage, receipt and statement are compiled
  • Centre Security: Notify centre security of high harm offenders and trespass notices

IOC Support Role:

  • Obtain CCTV footage and provide to RSS officer
  • Complete additional event details in Auror if required
  • Assist with evidence compilation and quality checking
IOC

When IOC Spots Incident/Offender (via CCTV monitoring)

IOC Responsibilities:

  • Auror Report: Create complete Auror event with all incident details
  • CCTV Evidence: Obtain and attach CCTV footage showing complete incident
  • Statement Generation: Generate formal statement as the person who reviewed footage
  • RSS Coordination: Direct RSS to collect training receipts and physical evidence

RSS Field Support Role:

  • Update Auror event with training receipt and product photos
  • Collect any additional physical evidence as directed
  • Report to police once footage, receipt and statement are compiled
  • Handle centre security notifications as required
STORE

Integrated Response Model

RSS officers operate within a coordinated response model where IOC provides remote oversight and RSS provides field response. Clear role definition prevents duplication of effort and ensures comprehensive incident management. Success depends on understanding when IOC leads, when RSS leads, and when coordination is required.

Core Operational Principle

Whoever spots the incident/offender determines the primary responsibility pathway, but both IOC and RSS have specific, non-negotiable duties regardless of who identifies the situation.

Standard Radio Protocol - CLEAR, CONCISE & CALM

All radio communications must follow professional protocols to ensure effective coordination and maintain operational security.

Basic Radio Procedures

1

Shift Commencement Check

First step when commencing shift: call BASE (the IOC) and check communications are working properly.

2

Proper Identification

Always state your store name first - if working at Highpoint: "Highpoint to Base" for clear identification.

3

Response Protocol

Base will respond: "Base copies, go ahead" - wait for this acknowledgment before continuing.

4

Acknowledgment Requirements

Every call needs acknowledgment: "Base copies" or "Highpoint copies" to confirm message received.

5

Communication Discipline

Two people cannot talk at same time - do NOT hog radio waves. Wait for acknowledgment before continuing.

Radio Call Structure

Precise Communication Requirements

What do you see - specific, factual observations
WHY is it important - threat level and urgency
Where are they NOW - exact location in store
What direction are they headed - movement patterns
Are you maintaining visual - current surveillance status
Do you need someone to take the BAY - backup requirements

Radio Communication Key Terms

Base

The IOC - Intelligence Operations Centre providing support and coordination.

Your Store

Your call sign - use store name for identification (e.g., "Highpoint to Base").

"The Bay"

When you have visual on subject - maintaining direct observation.

"Out of Work"

When you don't have visual - lost sight of subject or person of interest.

"Holding"

When stationed somewhere - maintaining position for observation or deterrence.

"Copy"

Acknowledgment - "Base copies" or "Highpoint copies" to confirm message received.

What Not To Do - Strictly Prohibited Activities

Critical Safety Prohibitions

RSS officers must NOT engage in the following activities under any circumstances:

⌧

Physical Intervention

Use physical intervention except when required for the removal of a person where the immediate safety of the store team and customers requires this. Any physical action must be in accordance with relevant laws and legislation.

⌧

Physical Apprehension

Chase, touch, handle, stop, detain, or apprehend suspected shoplifters. MA Services has a strict no physical apprehension policy.

⌧

Unauthorised Checking

Check customers' bags or receipts unless store has correct signage and conditions of entry clearly displayed and legally compliant.

⌧

Customer Banning

Issue verbal bans or trespass notices. Only Store Manager can issue trespass notices per Coles Trespass Notice procedure.

⌧

Security Protocols

Take possession of store keys, passwords, or PIN codes without explicit management permission and documented authorisation.

⌧

Weapons/Restraints

Carry weapons or restraining devices including batons, handcuffs, ties, or any other restraint equipment.

⌧

Unauthorised Documentation

Take photos or share electronic/social media content that is Coles property or make public comments about Coles operations.

Physical Intervention Guidelines

Critical Legal and Safety Clarification

Physical contact with individuals is generally prohibited to ensure safety and avoid escalation. However, physical intervention may be justified when required for the removal of a person where the immediate safety of store team and customers requires this action.

Important Legal Requirements:

  • Any physical action must be in accordance with relevant laws and legislation
  • Physical intervention should only occur when absolutely necessary for immediate safety
  • RSS officers must understand their legal authorities and limitations in their jurisdiction
  • All physical interventions must be immediately reported and documented
  • When in doubt, prioritise de-escalation and withdrawal while summoning appropriate assistance

Remember: The primary goal is always prevention and de-escalation. Physical intervention carries significant risks and legal implications.

Safety Priority Reminder

Fundamental Safety Principle

Your safety and the safety of others is of paramount importance.
Always prioritise de-escalation and follow proper escalation procedures. When in doubt, withdraw from dangerous situations and seek immediate assistance through proper channels.
